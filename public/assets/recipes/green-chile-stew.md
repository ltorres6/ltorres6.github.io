# Classic New Mexican Green Chile Stew

A hearty and flavorful stew featuring roasted green chiles, tender pork, and potatoes. A staple of New Mexican cuisine that brings warmth and comfort to any meal.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs pork shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 6-8 roasted green chiles, peeled and chopped
- 3 medium potatoes, diced
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil

## Instructions

1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
2. Brown the pork cubes on all sides, about 8-10 minutes total.
3. Add onion and garlic, cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
4. Add green chiles, cumin, and oregano. Stir to combine.
5. Pour in broth and bring to a boil.
6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.
7. Add diced potatoes and continue cooking for 30 minutes until tender.
8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
9. Serve hot with warm tortillas or bread.

## Notes

For best flavor, use Hatch green chiles when in season. Can be made ahead and reheated. The stew actually tastes better the next day as the flavors meld together.
